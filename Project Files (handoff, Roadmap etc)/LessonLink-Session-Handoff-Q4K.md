## What Happened

### 1. LessonLink Status Clarified

Captain corrected the assumption that LL was still mid-build. Phases 17, 17C (now 18-D scoreboard), 18, onboarding, and email are all **done**. Sonnet 4.6 (working in VSCode) had bloated the Q4J roadmap with running commentary, redundant file lists, and narrative debris. **LL is functionally complete.** What remains is testing, debugging, and polish — not new features.

### 2. Petland Reframed

Petland is no longer described as "just a reward system." It **is** the student progress system. XP tracks quality of performance. For the L, it gamifies learning so they stop thinking about "doing English" and start thinking about growing their Pet and engaging with the Kiddoland experience.

### 3. Three Projects Defined

After a long brainstorm (including one rightful slap from Captain for sycophantic throat-clearing), the three post-LL projects were codified:

**Project 1 — The Money Strand (TMS).** Fastest path to income. Five possible channels:
1. Learner Procurement (fastest, no build)
2. LessonLink SaaS (highest ceiling, big build)
3. Production Company revenue (narrow but on-brand)
4. Kiddoland as School (hire teachers, downstream of procurement)
5. Curriculum-as-Product (sell the method itself — closest to BARS)

**Project 2 — Production Company Strand (PCS).** Kiddoland Studios. Write graded readers, animated videos, RPG destination packs. RPG practice already exists with real learners. Travel Agent in Petland is nearly free to build (Pet Shop clone). Destination Packs are the product. Two distribution modes: inside LL for own learners, standalone for other teachers.

**Project 3 — Become A Real Strand (BARS).** Long-term legitimization via publisher. Three possible outputs: academic (theory), quasi-academic (methodology book), commercial (licensed by publisher or original Kiddoland reader series).
